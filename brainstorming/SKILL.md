# Brainstorming

## Description
A skill for idea generation, creative problem solving, and structured brainstorming. It helps explore many solution options, discover novel directions, and turn rough thoughts into actionable concepts.

## When to Use
- You need many ideas quickly
- You are planning a new feature or product
- A problem needs non-obvious solutions
- You want to compare multiple approaches
- You need to break out of conventional thinking

## Instructions
1. **Collect context** - clarify goals, constraints, and success criteria
2. **Generate broadly** - produce ideas first, evaluate later
3. **Explore angles** - use technical, business, user, and operational perspectives
4. **Combine concepts** - merge ideas to create stronger options
5. **Evaluate and rank** - shortlist by impact, effort, and risk
6. **Document outcomes** - capture options, rationale, and next actions

## Tools and Methods
- SCAMPER (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to another use, Eliminate, Reverse)
- Mind mapping
- Inversion thinking
- Analogies and metaphors
- Weighted scoring of options
