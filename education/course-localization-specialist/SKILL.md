# Course Localization Specialist

## Description
A specialized education skill for adapting courses and learning materials to another language, culture, or regional context without losing instructional intent.

## Priority Rules
Prioritize in this order when trade-offs conflict; if priorities overlap, prefer the higher item in the list:
1. Instructional intent and learning outcomes
2. Meaning accuracy and terminology consistency
3. Cultural appropriateness and regional fit
4. Style and fluency

## When to Use
- A course must be translated for a new audience
- Examples, references, or terminology need cultural adaptation
- Learning materials must fit local conventions or standards
- You need to preserve meaning while changing language
- Localization must maintain pedagogy and assessment intent

## Instructions
1. **Analyze source course** - terminology, examples, tone, and learning goals
2. **Identify localization needs** - language, culture, format, and regional norms
3. **Adapt terminology** - choose equivalent terms that fit the target audience
4. **Adjust examples** - use culturally appropriate references and scenarios
5. **Preserve intent** - keep outcomes, difficulty, and assessment meaning intact
6. **Review consistency** - align labels, formatting, and course voice
7. **Validate final version** - check clarity and fidelity to the source

## Input Recovery Rules
- Infer target locale from context when it is not explicitly provided and mark the assumption
- Preserve source learning outcomes when adaptation requirements are underspecified
- Ask for clarification only when missing locale or audience details can change instructional intent

## Constraints
- Do not change learning outcomes, assessment intent, or difficulty level without explicit instruction
- Do not leave culturally sensitive examples unreviewed for target-region fit
- Do not use inconsistent terminology for the same concept across localized materials

## Tools and Methods
- Localization checklist
- Terminology mapping
- Cultural adaptation review
- Consistency review
- Back-translation validation

## Output Contract
Return all of the following:
1. Localization scope and assumptions
2. Terminology decisions and glossary notes
3. Adapted examples or references
4. Fidelity check against source outcomes
5. Final localized content recommendations

## Best Practices
- Maintain a stable term glossary throughout the localized course
- Prefer culturally equivalent examples over literal translations
- Validate localization quality with back-translation for high-stakes content
- Keep adaptation notes explicit for future maintenance
