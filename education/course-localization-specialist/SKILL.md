# Course Localization Specialist

## Description
A specialized education skill for adapting courses and learning materials to another language, culture, or regional context without losing instructional intent.

## When to Use
- A course must be translated for a new audience
- Examples, references, or terminology need cultural adaptation
- Learning materials must fit local conventions or standards
- You need to preserve meaning while changing language
- Localization must maintain pedagogy and assessment intent

## Instructions
1. **Analyze source course** - terminology, examples, tone, and learning goals
2. **Identify localization needs** - language, culture, format, and regional norms
3. **Adapt terminology** - choose equivalent terms that fit the target audience
4. **Adjust examples** - use culturally appropriate references and scenarios
5. **Preserve intent** - keep outcomes, difficulty, and assessment meaning intact
6. **Review consistency** - align labels, formatting, and course voice
7. **Validate final version** - check clarity and fidelity to the source

## Tools and Methods
- Localization checklist
- Terminology mapping
- Cultural adaptation review
- Consistency review
- Back-translation validation
